## üéØ Exercise Set 5: Critical Thinking

### Question 1: Why Does Data Type Matter?

**Scenario**: You're tracking customer ages.

If you store age as:
- **Quantitative (number)**: 25, 30, 45, 22
- **Qualitative (category)**: "Young", "Middle-aged", "Senior"

**Question**: What can you do with quantitative age data that you CAN'T do with categorical age data?

**Your Answer**:

_________________________________________________

_________________________________________________

<details>
<summary>Sample Answer</summary>

With quantitative age data you can:
- Calculate average age
- Find median age
- Determine age range
- Sort precisely by age
- Identify specific age groups (e.g., 25-30)

With categorical data you only know general groups but can't calculate exact statistics.

</details>
