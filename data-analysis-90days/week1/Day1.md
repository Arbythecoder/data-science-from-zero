## ğŸ“Š Part 2: Types of Data

Understanding data types helps you choose the right analysis method.

### 1ï¸âƒ£ Quantitative Data (Numbers - You Can Measure)

**Definition**: Data that can be counted or measured.

#### Two Types:

**A) Discrete Data** (Can be counted, whole numbers)
- Number of students: 30
- Number of products sold: 150
- Number of clicks: 523

**B) Continuous Data** (Can be measured, decimals possible)
- Height: 5.7 feet
- Temperature: 28.5Â°C
- Price: â‚¦1,250.50

#### Examples:
```
Age: 25 (discrete - you can't be 25.5 years old in everyday terms)
Height: 5.8 feet (continuous - you can be 5.82 feet)
Test score: 85% (could be discrete or continuous depending on grading)
```

---

### 2ï¸âƒ£ Qualitative Data (Categories/Descriptions - You Can Describe)

**Definition**: Data that describes qualities or characteristics.

#### Two Types:

**A) Nominal Data** (Categories with no order)
- Colors: Red, Blue, Green
- Cities: Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt
- Product types: Electronics, Clothing, Food

**B) Ordinal Data** (Categories with order)
- Ratings: Poor, Fair, Good, Excellent
- Education: High School, Bachelor's, Master's, PhD
- Size: Small, Medium, Large

#### Examples:
```
Favorite color: "Blue" (nominal - no natural order)
Customer satisfaction: "Very Satisfied" (ordinal - has order)
Gender: "Male/Female" (nominal)
Star rating: 1-5 stars (ordinal - 5 is better than 1)
```
